  “The sea is in front of us and soldiers behind us!” cried another.

  The crowd quieted, though children still whimpered and horses still thundered in the distance.

  “Do not be afraid,” Moses said, his voice strong now. “Stand firm, and you will see how the Lord saves you today! The
  Egyptians you see now—you will not see them again.”

  A young girl near the front blinked. “How can we go forward? There’s water!”

  Moses pointed toward the sea. “The Lord will fight for us. We must trust Him.”

  He lifted his staff high over the Red Sea.

  At first, nothing happened.

  Then a wind began to blow.

  Not a gentle breeze—this was a mighty wind that roared like a lion. It whipped cloaks and scarves, bent palm trees, and
  drove spray from the waves. All night it blew. The people wrapped their arms around themselves and watched in awe as
  the sea began to change.

  The water pulled back.

  It split.

  A pathway of wet earth appeared where water had been moments before. On both sides, walls of water rose and shimmered
  in the moonlight, higher than any palace in Egypt.

  Eli gasped, eyes wide. “Mama… the sea is standing up!”

  His mother’s voice shook. “A miracle.”

  Miriam laughed in wonder. “He made a road in the sea!”

  Moses lowered his staff and shouted, “Go! Walk through! Keep moving!”

  The people stepped forward carefully at first, then with growing courage. Sand squished under sandals. Carts rattled.
  Babies cried. Sheep bleated. Grandparents leaned on staffs while older children helped carry bundles.

  As they entered the path, many looked up at the walls of water and hurried faster.

  A man whispered to his wife, “I can see fish in there!”

  A little girl reached out a hand before her father pulled it back. “No touching the water,” he said, trying to sound
  brave. “Just keep walking.”

  All night they crossed.

  At the rear, Moses and a few strong men guided the slowest travelers. Above them, a pillar of cloud moved behind the
  Israelites, standing between them and the Egyptian army like a giant shield.

  On the far side of the sea, the first families climbed onto dry land. They turned and watched as more and more people
  emerged, tired but alive.

  Eli stumbled onto the shore and flopped into the sand, laughing with relief. “We made it!”

  His mother kissed his forehead. “Yes, we made it.”

  But then came a shout from the back.

  “The Egyptians! They’re entering the path!”

  Pharaoh had not given up. His chariots charged into the seabed road, wheels pounding the earth. Soldiers yelled and
  cracked whips, trying to catch the escaping people.

  Pharaoh stood in his chariot and pointed his sword. “Forward! They are trapped now!”

  One of his captains hesitated. “My lord… this is no ordinary road. Their God—”

  “Forward!” Pharaoh roared.

  The chariots raced in.

  But soon the ground turned sticky and uneven. Wheels sank and twisted. Horses screamed and reared.

  A soldier cried out, “The wheels are breaking!”

  Another shouted, “Turn back! Their God is fighting us!”

  On the far shore, the Israelites watched, breathless. Some clung to each other. Some prayed aloud.

  Moses stood at the water’s edge, staff in hand, waiting.

  Then God spoke to him again:

  Stretch out your hand over the sea, so the waters may return.

  Moses raised his staff one more time.

  The wind stopped.

  For one heartbeat, all was silent.

  Then the walls of water crashed down with a thunder like mountains breaking. Waves roared over the chariots, over
  horses, over armor and spears. The sea closed and rolled, deep and wide as before.

  When the sun rose, the shore glowed gold. The enemy was gone. The people were safe.

  For a moment, no one moved.

  Then a single voice began to sing—Miriam’s.

  “Sing to the Lord, for He is highly exalted!”

  Women gathered around her with tambourines, and soon the whole camp joined in. Their voices rose over the water, joyful
  and free.

  “The horse and rider He has thrown into the sea!”

  Children danced. Old men laughed. Mothers cried happy tears. Fathers lifted toddlers into the air.

  Eli looked up at Moses, who stood quietly nearby, eyes shining with gratitude.

  “Did you know God would do that?” Eli asked.

  Moses knelt so he was eye level with the boy. “I knew God would make a way,” he said softly. “I just didn’t know what
  it would look like.”

  Eli thought about that. “So… when we’re scared, we trust Him first?”

  Moses smiled. “Yes. We listen, we obey, and we move forward.”

  Miriam came over, still holding her tambourine. “Come dance, Eli! Today is a day to remember forever.”

  Eli grinned and ran to the circle of singers.

  As the people celebrated, Moses looked across the sea one last time. Behind them was slavery, fear, and chains. Ahead
  was a long journey through the wilderness—but also hope, promise, and a future God would guide them to step by step.

  He turned toward the path ahead and called out, “People of Israel, gather your families. We go forward together.”

  The people answered with one voice:

  “Together!”

  And under the bright morning sky, they began walking into freedom.

  Moses’ big sister, Miriam, watched from a distance.

  Soon, Pharaoh’s daughter came down to the river with her servants.

  “What is that in the reeds?” she asked.

  A servant pulled out the basket. Inside was a crying baby.

  Pharaoh’s daughter’s face softened. “This is one of the Hebrew children.”

  Miriam quickly stepped forward. “Should I find a Hebrew woman to nurse the baby for you?”

  Pharaoh’s daughter nodded. “Yes. Bring her.”

  So Miriam brought their own mother. Jochebed cared for Moses until he was older, then Moses went to live in Pharaoh’s
  palace. He grew up wearing fine clothes and learning the ways of Egypt. But deep inside, he knew he was Hebrew.

  One day, Moses went out and saw an Egyptian slave master beating a Hebrew worker.

  soon?”
